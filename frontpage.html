✨ "Don't Pick the Wrong Yarn!✨

Milk Cotton Yarn

Milk Cotton Yarn

💡BEST USED AND TEXTURE

  • Texture: Velvety, smooth, and slightly stretchy.
  • Best used for: Baby clothes, beanies, blankets, and plush amigurumi toys.

Pure Cotton Yarn

Pure Cotton Yarny

💡BEST USED AND TEXTURE

  • Texture: Matte, firm, clean, and completely non-fuzzy.
  • Best used for: Tote bags, market bags, coasters, dishcloths, and water bottle holders.

Chenille / Velvet Yarn

Chenille / Velvet Yarn

💡BEST USED AND TEXTURE

  • Texture: Super plush, thick, and feels exactly like a luxury velvet blanket.
  • Best used for: Giant amigurumi (huge plushies), chunky throw blankets, and cozy floor pillow

Bamboo Yarn

Bamboo Yarn

💡BEST USED AND TEXTURE

  • Texture: Silky, cool to the touch, and has a beautiful, heavy fluid "drape" .
  • Best used for: Summer garments, lightweight cardigans, shawls, and premium baby wear.

T-Shirt Yarn

T-Shirt Yarn

💡BEST USED AND TEXTURE

  • Texture: Thick, wide, flat, and highly elastic.
  • Best used for: Sturdy storage baskets, home rugs, and structured handbags that need to stand upright.

🪄 Basic Crochet Terms & Abbreviations

Short Form Full Meaning & Explanation
1. The Basic Stitches
ch Chain
sc Single Crochet
hdc Half Double Crochet
dc Double Crochet
tc / tr Treble Crochet
sl st Slip Stitch
2. Starting & Ending
MR Magic Ring / Magic Circle
st / sts Stitch / Stitches
sp Space
sk Skip
FO Fasten Off
3. Shaping
inc Increase
dec Decrease
tog Together
4. Placement
BLO Back Loop Only
FLO Front Loop Only
YO Yarn Over

📌 Quick Example :

If a pattern says: "R1: 6 sc in MR"

Meaning: Round 1: Work 6 single crochets into a Magic Ring.

If a pattern says: "R2: inc in each st"

Meaning: Round 2: Work an increase (2 stitches) into every single stitch from the previous row.
